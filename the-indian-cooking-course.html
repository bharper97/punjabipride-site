The Indian Cooking Course

Techniques – Masterclasses by Monisha Bharadwaj

In-Depth Review: The Indian Cooking Course

Monisha Bharadwaj’s The Indian Cooking Course: Techniques – Masterclasses is an authoritative guide covering over 300 recipes, 40 step-by-step masterclasses, and comprehensive techniques to help both beginners and experienced cooks master authentic Indian cuisine. With vivid photography and detailed explanations, it goes beyond mere recipes—offering cultural context, insights into regional traditions, and healthy cooking principles rooted in Ayurveda. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

About the Author

Monisha Bharadwaj is an award-winning author, television personality, and chef. She regularly contributes to food magazines, appears on UK television and radio (including BBC’s Food and Drink and ITV’s Inside the Factory), and runs the Cooking With Monisha cookery school in London. Her extensive experience ensures that each technique is presented clearly, with precise tips on spice blending, ingredient selection, and cooking methods. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Book Structure & Key Features

Released in 2016, this 496-page hardcover is divided into thematic chapters focused on:

  • Rice, Flours & Breads: Learn to prepare perfect rice every time, create various flours, and master flatbreads such as chapatis and parathas.
  • Meat, Poultry & Seafood: Understand marinating techniques, grilling methods, and how to cook meats to tender perfection.
  • Lentils, Beans & Vegetables: Explore hearty dals, vegetable curries, and healthy side dishes reflecting India’s regional diversity.
  • Snacks, Street Foods & Sides: Step-by-step masterclasses on favorites like samosas, pakoras, and chaat, complete with assembly tips.
  • Chutneys, Pickles & Relishes: Craft flavorful condiments and accompaniments that elevate each meal.
  • Desserts & Drinks: From classic gulab jamun to cooling lassis, learn how to balance flavors and textures in traditional sweet dishes.

Each chapter features vivid, full-color photographs and detailed instructions to demystify complex techniques, making it accessible even if you’ve never cooked Indian food before. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Why It Stands Out

- Masterclasses: Over 40 hands-on tutorials (e.g., rolling perfectly shaped chapatis, frying crisp samosas, making silky-smooth chutneys).
- Ingredient Deep-Dives: Detailed explanations of staple spices, oils, and produce—plus tips on selecting and storing them.
- Regional Insights: Vivid coverage of how North Indian lentil dishes differ from South Indian coconut-based curries, contextualizing each recipe within its cultural backdrop. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Comprehensive scope—spans all major categories of Indian cooking.
  • High-quality photography makes each step clear and inspiring.
  • Strong focus on healthy cooking principles with Ayurvedic influence.

Cons:

  • Large hardcover can be heavy; not ideal for counter use.
  • Some recipes use specialized ingredients that may require sourcing online.

Who Should Read It?

- Home cooks eager to deepen their understanding of Indian techniques.
- Food enthusiasts looking to explore regional Indian flavors and learn from a seasoned instructor.
- Experienced chefs wanting a reference that ties cultural context to culinary techniques.
