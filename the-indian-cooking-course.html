The Indian Cooking Course

Techniques – Masterclasses by Monisha Bharadwaj

In-Depth Review:
The Indian Cooking Course

A concise look at Monisha Bharadwaj’s guide to mastering authentic Indian cuisine.

Monisha Bharadwaj’s The Indian Cooking Course: Techniques – Masterclasses is a step-by-step roadmap to over 300 recipes and 40 hands-on tutorials. Whether you’re a complete beginner or an experienced home cook, this 496-page volume demystifies Indian techniques, spices, and regional flavours with clear instructions, vivid photography, and a healthy Ayurvedic angle.

About the Author

Monisha Bharadwaj is an award-winning chef, TV personality, and founder of the Cooking With Monisha cookery school in London. You may recognize her from BBC’s Food and Drink or ITV’s Inside the Factory. She knows exactly how to break down each step—spice blending, ingredient prep, temperature control—so you never feel lost in the kitchen.

Book Structure & Key Features

Published in 2016, this hardcover volume is divided into six core sections. Each chapter features:

  • Rice, Flours & Breads: Perfect basmati rice, homemade flours, and rolling flawless chapatis or parathas.
  • Meat, Poultry & Seafood: Marinades, grilling techniques, and foolproof cooking methods for tender, flavourful proteins.
  • Lentils, Beans & Vegetables: Hearty dals, vibrant curries, and seasonal veggie dishes reflecting regional diversity.
  • Snacks, Street Foods & Sides: Step-by-step tutorials for samosas, pakoras, chaat, and other crowd-pleasers with plating tips.
  • Chutneys, Pickles & Relishes: Flavor-packed condiments that turn any meal into a feast.
  • Desserts & Drinks: Balancing sweetness and texture—from gulab jamun to cooling lassis.

Every recipe is accompanied by full-color photography and sidebars explaining spice origins, Ayurvedic benefits, and alternative ingredient suggestions.

Why It Stands Out

  • 40+ Masterclasses: Hands-on tutorials like rolling perfect chapatis, frying crisp samosas, and whisking silky chutneys.
  • Ingredient Deep-Dive: Comprehensive spice guides, oil recommendations, and tips on sourcing fresh produce.
  • Regional Context: North vs South—how a Tamil Nadu coconut curry differs from a Punjabi dal, with cultural anecdotes woven in.

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Encyclopedic: Covers every corner of Indian home cooking in one volume.
  • Visual Learning: High-quality photos make each step easy to follow.
  • Health-Focused: Integrates Ayurvedic principles and wholesome ingredients.

Cons:

  • Weight & Size: The 496-page hardcover can be hefty; not ideal for kitchen counters unless you have a stand.
  • Specialty Ingredients: A few recipes call for ingredients that aren’t in every grocery store (though substitutes are suggested).

Who Should Read It?

  • Beginners: Anyone new to Indian cooking who needs detailed, foolproof guidance.
  • Home Chefs: Cooks wanting to level up with authentic regional techniques.
  • Food Enthusiasts: Readers curious about cultural context, health benefits, and visual storytelling.
